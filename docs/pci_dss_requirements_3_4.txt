PCI-DSS Requirements 3 and 4 — Data Protection and Encryption

Requirement 3.1 — Data Retention Policy
Cardholder data storage must be kept to the minimum necessary. A data retention and disposal policy must be implemented. Data must be purged when no longer needed for business or legal reasons. All disposal must be documented.

Requirement 3.2 — Sensitive Authentication Data
Sensitive authentication data must not be stored after authorization even if encrypted. This includes full track data, card verification codes, and PINs. These data elements must be rendered unrecoverable if accidentally stored.

Requirement 3.3 — Mask PAN
The primary account number must be masked when displayed. Only personnel with a legitimate business need can see the full PAN. The first six and last four digits are the maximum number of digits that may be displayed.

Requirement 3.4 — Render PAN Unreadable
The primary account number must be rendered unreadable anywhere it is stored. Acceptable methods include strong one-way hash functions, truncation, index tokens with secure pads, and strong cryptography with key management processes.

Requirement 3.5 — Key Management
Cryptographic keys used for encryption of cardholder data must be protected against disclosure and misuse. Key management procedures must include generation, distribution, storage, access, retirement, and destruction of keys.

Requirement 4.1 — Strong Cryptography in Transit
Strong cryptography must be used to safeguard cardholder data during transmission over open public networks. Only trusted and accepted keys and certificates must be accepted. TLS 1.2 or higher is the minimum requirement for all transmissions.

Requirement 4.2 — No Unprotected PANs
Primary account numbers must never be sent via end-user messaging technologies such as email, instant messaging, SMS, or chat unless all required elements of the message are encrypted.
